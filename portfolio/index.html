The Farmers Market


The Farmers Market was the capstone project during my time at The Iron Yard. I've been bartending for the past two years and one of my customers, a local chef, gave me the idea for the app. He talked about how he wished there was a way for local farmers to put their crops online, so then local chef's and restaurants would be able to look for the foods they wanted. The farm to table movement is growing and the aim of this app was to aid in that growth.

Genius Sports

Genius Sports is a youth sports team management app developed for a hackathon at The Iron Yard. My team included two other Ruby on Rails students for the back end, as well as front end and iOS mobile students. We had thirty six hours to design and implement an app to be judged by the team at SOLTECH, who also sponsored the event and supplied the projects for the hackathon. This app was a lot of fun to make and also taught me a great deal about working in a team while developing software.

Todo List

This is a simple Rails CRUD app for making todo lists. It helped to re-inforce the MVC concepts going behind the scenes in Rails. The app allows users to create todo lists and add todo items for each list. The user can also edit and delete any list of their own. The app uses Devise for authentication. Bootstrap was used for the styling, utilizing a Bootswatch theme

Raddit


Raddit is a Hacker News and Reddit style user driven site that allows users to add links for news, sports, etc. This app was a tutorial I did on my own to gain more experience with Ruby on Rails. Specically using the Rails scaffold command, as well as using the acts_as_votable and simple_form gems. Bootstrap was added for styling.
